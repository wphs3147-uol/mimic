Sophie sat by the window, watching the rain paint streaks against the glass. The old clock on the wall chimed midnight. She should have been asleep, but something felt… off.

A soft knock echoed through the quiet house. Her heart skipped. Who would visit at this hour? Slowly, she tiptoed to the door and pressed her ear against it. Silence.

Another knock. This time, more urgent.

“Sophie…” a whisper called.

She froze. The voice was familiar—too familiar. But that was impossible. He had been gone for years.

Hands trembling, she turned the knob and peeked outside. The porch was empty. Only the rain greeted her, dancing in the dim streetlight.

Then, just as she closed the door, she heard it again.

“Sophie, let me in.”
